# IP-S — Overview

> IP-S is the most widely used resist on the GT2 for general-purpose 3D microprinting. If you are new to the machine and do not have a specific application requirement, IP-S with the 25x objective is the right starting point.

---

## What Is IP-S?

**IP-S** is a medium-viscosity photopolymer resin designed for use with the **25x objective** in DiLL mode. The "S" designation stands for "standard" — it was formulated to be the workhorse resist for the GT2 platform.

IP-S is characterized by:
- **Very low polymerization shrinkage** — one of the lowest in the IP-series
- **Smooth surface finish** — produces surfaces with low roughness compared to IP-Q
- **Balanced resolution and speed** — not as fine as IP-Dip2, but much faster to print large structures
- **Broad substrate compatibility** — works on ITO glass, silicon, quartz, and other surfaces

---

## Key Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Refractive index (n) at 780 nm | 1.478 |
| Recommended objective | 25x (NA 0.8) |
| Printing mode | DiLL |
| Minimum lateral feature size | ~500 nm (typical: ~1 um in practice) |
| Developer | PGMEA, followed by IPA rinse |
| Appearance | Clear, colorless, slightly viscous liquid |
| Viscosity | Medium (~300-500 mPa·s) |
| Shrinkage (linear) | ~2-4% (very low) |

---

## When to Use IP-S

Use IP-S when:

- You need structures in the 1 um to ~1 mm size range
- Surface quality matters (smooth walls, low roughness)
- You are making mechanical structures, molds, or templates
- You need a well-characterized resist with abundant published literature
- This is your first print on the GT2 (IP-S is the most forgiving and predictable resist)
- Your substrate is ITO-coated glass (the standard for 25x DiLL)

Do NOT use IP-S when:

- You need sub-500 nm features (use IP-Dip2 instead)
- You need biocompatibility (use IP-Visio)
- You need flexible output (use IP-PDMS)
- You need mm-scale fast printing (consider IP-Q)
