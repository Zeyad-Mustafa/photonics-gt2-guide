# IP-Visio — Overview

> IP-Visio is Nanoscribe's biocompatible resist. If your printed structures will come into contact with living cells, tissue, or biological fluids, IP-Visio is the correct material choice.

---

## What Is IP-Visio?

**IP-Visio** is a photopolymer resist formulated for **biomedical and life science applications**. It shares the same 25x/DiLL workflow as IP-S but is distinguished by:

- **Biocompatibility** — tested and verified as non-cytotoxic (does not kill cells when structures are properly developed and cured)
- **Very low autofluorescence** — critical for fluorescence microscopy; IP-S and IP-Q have moderate fluorescence backgrounds that can interfere with fluorescent imaging
- **Optical clarity** — low absorption across the visible spectrum

IP-Visio is used wherever the printed structure must not harm biological systems or interfere with fluorescence imaging.

---

## Key Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Refractive index (n) | ~1.48 |
| Recommended objective | 25x (NA 0.8) |
| Printing mode | DiLL |
| Biocompatibility | Verified (ISO 10993 compliant after proper development) |
| Autofluorescence | Very low |
| Developer | PGMEA, followed by IPA rinse |
| Viscosity | Medium |

---

## When to Use IP-Visio

Use IP-Visio when:

- Printing cell scaffolds for 3D cell culture
- Making structures for tissue engineering
- Creating microfluidic devices that will carry cells or biological fluids
- Fluorescence imaging will be performed on or around the printed structure
- Any application requiring the printed material to be non-toxic to living systems

Do NOT use IP-Visio when:

- Biocompatibility is not required (use IP-S — it has more published parameters)
- You need sub-micron resolution (use IP-Dip2)
- You need flexible output (use IP-PDMS)

---

## Biocompatibility: Important Caveats

IP-Visio is biocompatible **only when properly developed and UV-cured**. Uncured or partially cured IP-Visio resin contains photoinitiators and monomers that are cytotoxic.

Before placing IP-Visio-printed structures in contact with cells:
1. Complete the full PGMEA + IPA development protocol
2. UV cure at 405 nm for the full recommended time (up to 20 minutes)
3. Allow the structure to degas overnight in a clean environment (ideally under UV in a sterile field)
4. Confirm no residual resist odor before biological use

Consult your institution's biosafety and materials testing protocols for any implant or in vivo application.
