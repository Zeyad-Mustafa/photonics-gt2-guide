# IP-PDMS — Handling and Storage

---

## Storage Requirements

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 2-8 C |
| Light protection | Required |
| Shelf life (unopened) | ~12 months |
| Shelf life (opened) | Use within 6 months |

IP-PDMS is more viscous than other IP-series resists. Allow it to warm fully to room temperature (30-60 minutes) before use. Cold IP-PDMS is very thick and difficult to dispense in controlled drops.

---

## Dispensing Notes

Because of its high viscosity:
- Use a pipette rather than relying on the dropper tip for controlled volumes
- A 5-15 uL drop is appropriate for 25x DiLL use
- The drop will not spread as readily as IP-S — this is expected
- Ensure the drop fully contacts the substrate with no air pocket between drop and surface

---

## PPE

| Item | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Gloves | Nitrile |
| Eye protection | Safety glasses |
| Lab coat | Standard |

PDMS-based materials have lower acute toxicity than pure acrylate resists but still contain photoinitiators. Standard acrylate handling precautions apply.

---

## Post-Print Handling

After development, IP-PDMS structures are:
- Soft and easily deformed — handle with care
- Sticky on the surface (PDMS is naturally tacky) — avoid contamination with particles
- Hydrophobic — water beads up on the surface; wet the structure with IPA before water-based rinsing

---

## Waste Disposal

- Liquid IP-PDMS: dispose as silicone/acrylate mixed chemical waste; label clearly
- Cured structures: solid chemical waste; check with EHS for silicone classification at your institution
