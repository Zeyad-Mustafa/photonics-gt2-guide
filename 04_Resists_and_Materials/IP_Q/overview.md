# IP-Q — Overview

> IP-Q is the resist for large, fast prints. When your structure is measured in millimeters rather than micrometers and print time is a real constraint, IP-Q with the 10x objective is the correct choice.

---

## What Is IP-Q?

**IP-Q** is a low-to-medium viscosity photopolymer resist formulated for use with the **10x objective** (NA 0.3) in DiLL mode. It is optimized for **high-speed, large-volume printing** where resolution is secondary to throughput.

The "Q" designation refers to its intended use case: quick, large-scale fabrication.

IP-Q characteristics:
- Designed for the longest working distance objective (10x, WD = 700 um)
- Compatible with large silicon substrates (4-inch wafer or diced pieces)
- Prints large voxels quickly — single print jobs can span millimeters in XY
- Lower resolution than IP-S or IP-Dip2, but that is the deliberate trade-off

---

## Key Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Refractive index (n) at 780 nm | 1.48 |
| Recommended objective | 10x (NA 0.3) |
| Printing mode | DiLL with Multi-DiLL Silicon Wafer Holder |
| Minimum lateral feature size | ~2-5 um (typical) |
| Maximum print field | ~1.5 x 1.5 mm (per field, stitchable) |
| Developer | PGMEA, followed by IPA rinse |
| Appearance | Clear, slightly yellow-tinted liquid |
| Viscosity | Low-medium |

---

## When to Use IP-Q

Use IP-Q when:

- Your structure is 500 um to several mm in XY
- Print speed is a priority
- Feature resolution of 2-5 um is acceptable
- Your substrate is silicon or a large flat surface
- You are making scaffolds, support structures, or bulk fills
- You are prototyping a geometry that you will later refine with IP-S or IP-Dip2

Do NOT use IP-Q when:

- Features smaller than ~2 um are required (use IP-S or IP-Dip2)
- Your substrate is ITO glass (use IP-S)
- You need the smoothest possible surface finish (IP-S is better)
- You are using any objective other than the 10x
