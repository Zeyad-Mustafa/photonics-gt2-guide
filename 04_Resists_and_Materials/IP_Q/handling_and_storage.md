# IP-Q — Handling and Storage

---

## Storage Requirements

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 2-8 C (refrigerator) |
| Light protection | Required — original dark vial or foil-wrapped |
| Shelf life (unopened) | ~12 months |
| Shelf life (opened) | Use within 6 months |

Allow 30 minutes at room temperature before use.

---

## PPE

| Item | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Gloves | Nitrile |
| Eye protection | Safety glasses |
| Lab coat | Standard |
| Work area | Fume hood recommended |

---

## Dispensing

IP-Q has lower viscosity than IP-S. The bottle dropper tip delivers approximately 10-20 uL per drop. Apply only 1-2 drops to the substrate — see **[drop_casting_technique.md](./drop_casting_technique.md)** for the critical details.

Do not return unused resist to the stock vial.

---

## Waste Disposal

- Liquid IP-Q: acrylate/photopolymer chemical waste
- PGMEA + dissolved IP-Q developer waste: halogen-free organic solvent waste

---

## Signs of Degradation

- Increased viscosity
- Dark yellow or brown color (normal IP-Q is only slightly yellow — deep yellow is degradation)
- Gel particles visible in the liquid
- Drops spread flat even on clean substrates (may indicate increased surface tension from degradation)
