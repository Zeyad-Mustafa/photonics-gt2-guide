# Textbooks and Review Articles

## Overview

This file lists the most useful textbooks and comprehensive review articles for GT2 users who want a deeper understanding of the underlying physics, chemistry, or fabrication landscape. These are the resources to turn to when the application-specific papers leave gaps, or when you need to explain the technique to collaborators from other fields.

---

## Textbooks

### Two-Photon Polymerization and Nanofabrication

Baldacchini, T. (editor)
Three-Dimensional Microfabrication Using Two-Photon Polymerization: Fundamentals, Technology, and Applications.
Elsevier, 2015. Second edition 2020.
ISBN: 978-0128178942

This is the definitive textbook on two-photon polymerization. It covers the full range of the field in a single volume: the physical and chemical basis of 2PP, laser system requirements, resin chemistry, parameter optimization, characterization methods, and application chapters covering optics, photonics, microfluidics, biomedical, and microrobotics. Written by leading researchers in each area. This book should be the first long-form reference any new GT2 user reads cover to cover. If your institution's library does not have a copy, request one.

---

### Nonlinear Optics (for Two-Photon Absorption Physics)

Boyd, R. W.
Nonlinear Optics.
Academic Press, 4th edition, 2020.
ISBN: 978-0128116975

The standard graduate textbook on nonlinear optics. Chapter 12 covers two-photon absorption specifically. Reading this chapter provides a rigorous foundation for understanding why the GT2 laser parameters (peak power, pulse duration, repetition rate) are what they are, and why the two-photon process confines polymerization to the focal volume. Essential for users who want a quantitative understanding rather than an empirical one.

---

### Photopolymer Chemistry

Andrzejewska, E.
Photopolymerization kinetics of multifunctional monomers.
Progress in Polymer Science, 26(4), 605-665, 2001.
DOI: 10.1016/S0079-6700(01)00004-1

A comprehensive review of the kinetics of free-radical photopolymerization, covering initiation, propagation, termination, oxygen inhibition, and the gel point transition. This is the best single reference for understanding the chemistry of what happens when the laser polymerizes the resin. Relevant for understanding shrinkage, underdevelopment, and the effect of oxygen on surface quality.

---

### Micro and Nanofabrication (Broader Context)

Madou, M. J.
Fundamentals of Microfabrication and Nanotechnology, Volume I, II, III.
CRC Press, 3rd edition, 2011.
ISBN: 978-0849331800

The most comprehensive reference on microfabrication broadly — photolithography, deposition, etching, MEMS, BioMEMS, and emerging techniques including 2PP. Useful for GT2 users who want to understand how 2PP fits within the broader microfabrication landscape, and for planning hybrid workflows that combine GT2 printing with conventional microfabrication steps.

## Comprehensive Review Articles

Malinauskas, M., Zukauskas, A., Hasegawa, S., Hayasaki, Y., Mizeikis, V., Buividas, R., and Juodkazis, S.
Ultrafast laser processing of materials: from science to industry.
Light: Science and Applications, 5, e16133, 2016.
DOI: 10.1038/lsa.2016.133

Broad review of ultrafast laser processing covering 2PP, laser ablation, surface structuring, and waveguide writing in glass. Useful for situating 2PP within the broader context of femtosecond laser fabrication and understanding what other processes your facility's ultrafast lasers might enable.

---

Ligon, S. C., Liska, R., Stampfl, J., Gurr, M., and Mulhaupt, R.
Polymers for 3D printing and customized additive manufacturing.
Chemical Reviews, 117(15), 10212-10290, 2017.
DOI: 10.1021/acs.chemrev.7b00074

Comprehensive review of photopolymers for additive manufacturing, covering resin chemistry, mechanical properties, biocompatibility, and process compatibility for all major photopolymerization-based 3D printing methods including 2PP. Essential background for understanding resin chemistry, how IP-series resins compare to other photopolymers, and where resin development is heading.

---

Farsari, M., Vamvakaki, M., and Chichkov, B. N.
Multiphoton polymerization of hybrid materials.
Journal of Optics, 12(12), 124001, 2010.
DOI: 10.1088/2040-8978/12/12/124001

Review of 2PP using hybrid organic-inorganic materials (such as OrmoComp and SZ2080), which have lower shrinkage and better thermal stability than pure organic resins. Relevant context for understanding the material space beyond standard IP-series resins and for applications requiring higher temperature stability or glass-like properties without sintering.

---

Ovsianikov, A., Viertl, J., Chichkov, B., Oubaha, M., MacCraith, B., Sakellari, I., Giakoumaki, A., Gray, D., Vamvakaki, M., Farsari, M., and Fotakis, C.
Ultra-low shrinkage hybrid photosensitive material for two-photon polymerization microfabrication.
ACS Nano, 2(11), 2257-2262, 2008.
DOI: 10.1021/nn800451w

Introduced the SZ2080 hybrid resin with sub-1 percent volumetric shrinkage, compared to 8 to 12 percent for standard acrylate resins. Provides the chemical basis for low-shrinkage resin design and is relevant background for understanding why IP-S was formulated for low shrinkage in optical applications.

## For Non-Expert Audiences

If you need to explain 2PP printing to a collaborator from biology, chemistry, or engineering who has no background in optics or nanofabrication, the following resources provide accessible introductions:

Maruo, S. and Fourkas, J. T.
Recent progress in multiphoton microfabrication.
Laser and Photonics Reviews, 2(1-2), 100-111, 2008.
DOI: 10.1002/lpor.200710039

A well-written overview of 2PP aimed at a broad photonics audience. Covers the physical mechanism, key results across applications, and outstanding challenges in accessible language. Good for sharing with collaborators who want to understand what the GT2 does without deep technical detail.
