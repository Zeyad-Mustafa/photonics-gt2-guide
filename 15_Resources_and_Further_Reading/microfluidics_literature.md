# Microfluidics Literature

## Overview

This file covers published work on microfluidic devices fabricated by two-photon polymerization, including channel networks, integrated mixers, droplet generators, and organ-on-chip systems.

---

## Foundational Microfluidics Fabrication Papers

Bhattacharjee, N., Urrios, A., Kang, S., and Folch, A.
The upcoming 3D-printing revolution in microfluidics.
Lab on a Chip, 16(10), 1720-1742, 2016.
DOI: 10.1039/C6LC00163G

A comprehensive review of 3D printing methods for microfluidics, including two-photon polymerization, stereolithography, inkjet, and extrusion approaches. Compares resolution, material options, and throughput across methods. Essential background reading before starting a microfluidic project — helps contextualize when the GT2 is the right tool versus other methods.

---

Sugioka, K. and Cheng, Y.
Femtosecond laser three-dimensional micro- and nanofabrication.
Applied Physics Reviews, 1(4), 041303, 2014.
DOI: 10.1063/1.4904320

Review of femtosecond laser fabrication for microfluidics and photonics, covering both 2PP polymer printing and femtosecond laser ablation in glass. Useful for understanding the full range of femtosecond-laser-based microfluidic fabrication options.

---

## 3D Channel Architectures

Amato, L., Gu, Y., Bellini, N., Eaton, S. M., Cerullo, G., and Osellame, R.
Integrated three-dimensional filter separates nanoscale from microscale elements in a microfluidic chip.
Lab on a Chip, 12(6), 1135-1142, 2012.
DOI: 10.1039/C2LC21116E

Demonstration of a 3D nanoscale filter integrated into a microfluidic chip by direct laser writing. The filter had pore sizes below 1 um, defined by the 3D printed polymer structure, for size-based separation of nanoparticles from microspheres. A good example of a device function that is impossible with planar photolithography.

---

Erkal, J. L., Selimovic, A., Gross, B. C., Lockwood, S. Y., Walton, E. L., McNamara, S., Martin, R. S., and Spence, D. M.
3D printed microfluidic devices with integrated versatile and reusable electrodes.
Lab on a Chip, 14(12), 2023-2032, 2014.
DOI: 10.1039/C4LC00171K

While focused on FDM printing rather than 2PP, this paper is widely cited for its discussion of integrating electrodes into 3D printed microfluidics and is useful background for electrical sensing integration.

## Passive Mixers

Stroock, A. D., Dertinger, S. K. W., Ajdari, A., Mezic, I., Stone, H. A., and Whitesides, G. M.
Chaotic mixer for microchannels.
Science, 295(5555), 647-651, 2002.
DOI: 10.1126/science.1066238

The foundational paper on herringbone (staggered herringbone) passive mixing in microfluidic channels. The herringbone groove geometry described here is directly applicable to GT2-printed channels — the grooves are printed on channel walls as part of the 3D structure. Essential reading for anyone designing passive mixing elements.

---

## Droplet Microfluidics

Anna, S. L., Bontoux, N., and Stone, H. A.
Formation of dispersions using flow focusing in microchannels.
Applied Physics Letters, 82(3), 364-366, 2003.
DOI: 10.1063/1.1537519

The foundational paper on flow-focusing droplet generation. The T-junction and flow-focusing geometries described here are among the most commonly printed GT2 microfluidic devices.

---

## Organ-on-Chip and Integrated Biological Devices

Huh, D., Matthews, B. D., Mammoto, A., Montoya-Zavala, M., Hsin, H. Y., and Ingber, D. E.
Reconstituting organ-level lung functions on a chip.
Science, 328(5986), 1662-1668, 2010.
DOI: 10.1126/science.1188302

The landmark organ-on-chip paper demonstrating a functional lung-on-chip. While fabricated by conventional PDMS soft lithography, this paper defines the organ-on-chip concept and performance benchmarks that GT2-printed devices in this space aspire to match or improve upon with 3D geometry.

---

Skylar-Scott, M. A., Mueller, J., Visser, C. W., and Lewis, J. A.
Voxelated soft matter via multimaterial multinozzle 3D printing.
Nature, 575, 330-335, 2019.
DOI: 10.1038/s41586-019-1736-8

Multi-material 3D printing of soft matter including vascular networks. Not GT2-specific, but highly relevant for the design of perfusable 3D tissue constructs and for understanding the state of the art in 3D-printed biological architectures.

## Surface Functionalization for Microfluidics

Goddard, J. M. and Hotchkiss, J. H.
Polymer surface modification for the attachment of bioactive compounds.
Progress in Polymer Science, 32(7), 698-725, 2007.
DOI: 10.1016/j.progpolymsci.2007.04.002

Review of surface modification strategies for polymers used in microfluidic and biological applications. Covers O2 plasma activation, silane coupling, and protein adsorption — the main methods used to functionalize GT2-printed IP-series surfaces for biological device applications.
