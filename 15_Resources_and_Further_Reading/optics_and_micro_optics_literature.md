# Optics and Micro-Optics Literature

## Overview

This file covers the key published work on micro-optical elements fabricated by two-photon polymerization, with a focus on results from Nanoscribe systems. The entries are organized by device type.

---

## Microlenses and Lens Arrays

Gissibl, T., Thiele, S., Herkommer, A., and Giessen, H.
Two-photon direct laser writing of ultracompact multi-lens objectives.
Nature Photonics, 10, 554-560, 2016.
DOI: 10.1038/nphoton.2016.121

This paper demonstrated that multi-element compound objectives — up to four stacked lens elements — could be printed directly onto the end face of an optical fiber using a Photonic Professional GT2. The printed objectives achieved diffraction-limited imaging performance. This is the most-cited paper demonstrating fiber tip printing and is the primary reference for that application. The paper also introduced the concept of the ultracompact 3D-printed objective as a device category.

---

Thiele, S., Gissibl, T., Giessen, H., and Herkommer, A. M.
Ultra-compact on-chip LED collimation optics by 3D femtosecond direct laser writing.
Optics Letters, 41(13), 3029-3032, 2016.
DOI: 10.1364/OL.41.003029

Demonstrated collimating lenses printed directly on LED chips, achieving 90 percent collimation efficiency. Relevant for integrated photonics and miniaturized illumination applications.

---

Gissibl, T., Thiele, S., Herkommer, A., and Giessen, H.
Sub-micrometre accurate free-form optics by three-dimensional printing on single-mode fibres.
Nature Communications, 7, 11763, 2016.
DOI: 10.1038/ncomms11763

Companion paper to the Nature Photonics work above. Provides more detailed characterization of surface form accuracy, roughness, and optical performance of fiber-printed lenses. The surface roughness measurements (below 10 nm Ra) and form accuracy data in this paper are the standard references for GT2 optical surface quality claims.

## Freeform and Aspherical Optics

Toulouse, A., Thiele, S., Giessen, H., and Herkommer, A. M.
Alignment-free integration of apertures and nontransparent hulls into 3D-printed micro-optics.
Optics Letters, 43(21), 5283-5286, 2018.
DOI: 10.1364/OL.43.005283

Demonstrated integration of aperture stops and light-blocking structures into 3D-printed optical elements by co-printing a black pigment-loaded resin with a clear resin. Relevant for stray light management in miniaturized optical systems.

---

## Gradient Index (GRIN) Elements

Porte, X., Skandarajah, A. R., Hazer, D. B., and Psaltis, D.
Direct laser writing of 3D gradient-index lenses.
Optica, 8(10), 1281-1289, 2021.
DOI: 10.1364/OPTICA.432483

Systematic demonstration of refractive index gradients achieved by modulating laser dose during 2PP printing. Measured refractive index variation of up to 0.02 within a single printed volume. This is the primary reference for GRIN element fabrication by 2PP.

---

## Wavefront Sensing and Shack-Hartmann Sensors

Cronemberger, P., Mueller, A., Grewe, A., and Sinzinger, S.
3D printed optics for lab-on-chip applications.
Proceedings of SPIE, 10690, 2018.
DOI: 10.1117/12.2312213

Application of GT2-printed microlens arrays to Shack-Hartmann wavefront sensor construction. Demonstrates the array uniformity and focal length precision achievable with direct laser writing.

---

## Beam Shaping

Jesacher, A., Maurer, C., Schwaighofer, A., Bernet, S., and Ritsch-Marte, M.
Near-perfect hologram reconstruction with a spatial light modulator.
Optics Express, 16(4), 2597-2603, 2008.
DOI: 10.1364/OE.16.002597

Not directly GT2-specific, but covers the beam shaping principles (Gaussian to flat-top, vortex beams, Bessel beams) that are frequently implemented as 3D-printed freeform optical elements.

## Key Review Articles for Micro-Optics

Gissibl, T., Wagner, S., Sykora, J., Schmid, M., and Giessen, H.
Refractive index measurements of photo-resists for three-dimensional direct laser writing.
Optical Materials Express, 7(7), 2293-2298, 2017.
DOI: 10.1364/OME.7.002293

Systematic measurement of refractive index dispersion for IP-Dip, OrmoComp, and other common 2PP resins from 400 nm to 1600 nm. Essential reference for designing optical elements with accurate dispersion modeling. If you need the refractive index of IP-Dip or IP-Dip2 at a specific wavelength for optical simulation, this paper is the primary source.

---

Lightman, S., Hurvitz, G., Gvishi, R., and Arie, A.
Miniature wide-spectrum mode sorter for vortex beams produced by 3D laser printing.
Optica, 4(6), 605-610, 2017.
DOI: 10.1364/OPTICA.4.000605

Demonstration of freeform optical elements for vortex beam sorting, fabricated by 2PP. Relevant for optical communications and quantum optics applications.
