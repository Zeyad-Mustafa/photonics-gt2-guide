# Biomedical Literature

## Overview

This file covers published work on biomedical applications of two-photon polymerization, including cell scaffolds, tissue engineering, mechanobiology, neural interfaces, and drug delivery. It also includes foundational biocompatibility studies for IP-series resins.

---

## Biocompatibility of 2PP Resins

Ovsianikov, A., Gruene, M., Pflaum, M., Koch, L., Maiorana, F., Wilhelmi, M., Haverich, A., and Chichkov, B.
Laser printing of cells into 3D scaffolds.
Biofabrication, 2(1), 014104, 2010.
DOI: 10.1088/1758-5082/2/1/014104

One of the earlier systematic studies of cell compatibility with 2PP-printed polymer scaffolds. Assessed cell viability, attachment, and proliferation on OrmoComp and similar acrylate-based resins. Sets the baseline for understanding what biocompatibility testing is required before using GT2 resins in cell experiments.

---

Farsari, M. and Chichkov, B. N.
Two-photon fabrication.
Nature Photonics, 3, 450-452, 2009.
DOI: 10.1038/nphoton.2009.131

Short review discussing the biomedical potential of 2PP fabrication, including scaffolds, drug delivery, and biosensing. Useful as a concise overview of the field for biomedical audiences unfamiliar with the fabrication technique.

## Cell Scaffolds and Tissue Engineering

Klein, F., Richter, B., Striebel, T., Franz, C. M., von Freymann, G., Wegener, M., and Bastmeyer, M.
Two-component polymer scaffolds for controlled three-dimensional cell culture.
Advanced Materials, 23(11), 1341-1345, 2011.
DOI: 10.1002/adma.201004060

Demonstrated multi-material 2PP scaffolds with regions of different stiffness to direct cell morphology and migration. One of the earlier demonstrations of using spatial mechanical heterogeneity in a printed scaffold to control cell behavior. Directly relevant to mechanobiology scaffold design.

---

Ovsianikov, A., Deiwick, A., Van Vlierberghe, S., Dubruel, P., Moller, L., Drager, G., and Chichkov, B.
Laser fabrication of three-dimensional CAD scaffolds from photosensitive gelatin for applications in tissue engineering.
Biomacromolecules, 12(4), 851-858, 2011.
DOI: 10.1021/bm1015305

2PP fabrication of scaffolds from gelatin methacryloyl — a biologically derived photopolymer. Relevant background for understanding the space of biocompatible resins beyond IP-series, and for degradable scaffold applications.

---

Raimondi, M. T., Eaton, S. M., Laganà, M., Aprile, V., Nava, M. M., Cerullo, G., and Osellame, R.
Three-dimensional structural niches engineered via two-photon laser polymerization promote stem cell homing.
Acta Biomaterialia, 9(1), 4579-4584, 2013.
DOI: 10.1016/j.actbio.2012.08.022

Demonstrated that scaffold geometry at the single-cell scale (niches sized to hold individual stem cells) can direct stem cell homing behavior. The niche dimensions were designed based on measured cell dimensions and printed by 2PP. Relevant for scaffold design in stem cell biology.

## Mechanobiology

Trappmann, B., Gautrot, J. E., Connelly, J. T., Strange, D. G. T., Li, Y., Oyen, M. L., Cohen Stuart, M. A., Boehm, H., Li, B., Vogel, V., Spatz, J. P., Watt, F. M., and Huck, W. T. S.
Extracellular-matrix tethering regulates stem-cell fate.
Nature Materials, 11, 642-649, 2012.
DOI: 10.1038/nmat3339

Demonstrated that substrate stiffness perceived by cells depends not just on bulk modulus but on the density of polymer tethering points available for cell attachment. Highly relevant for designing GT2 scaffolds where stiffness and surface chemistry are both design parameters.

---

Tan, J. L., Tien, J., Pirone, D. M., Gray, D. S., Bhadriraju, K., and Chen, C. S.
Cells lying on a bed of microneedles: An approach to isolate mechanical force.
Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 100(4), 1484-1489, 2003.
DOI: 10.1073/pnas.0235407100

The foundational paper on micropost arrays for traction force microscopy. Defined the technique of using deflection of elastic polymer posts to measure cell-generated forces. The micropost geometry described here is directly fabricated by GT2 printing with IP-PDMS in modern implementations.

## Neural Interfaces

Tian, B., Liu, J., Dvir, T., Jin, L., Tsui, J. H., Qing, Q., Suo, Z., Langer, R., Kohane, D. S., and Lieber, C. M.
Macroporous nanowire nanoelectronic scaffolds for synthetic tissues.
Nature Materials, 11, 986-994, 2012.
DOI: 10.1038/nmat3404

Demonstrates 3D electronic scaffolds for recording from neural tissue. Not 2PP-fabricated, but highly relevant for the design principles of 3D neural interfaces that GT2 structures can now implement.

---

## Drug Delivery

Tao, S. L. and Desai, T. A.
Microfabricated drug delivery systems: from particles to pores.
Advanced Drug Delivery Reviews, 55(3), 315-328, 2003.
DOI: 10.1016/S0169-409X(02)00227-2

Review of microfabricated drug delivery systems with controlled release geometries. Covers the design principles for pore size, wall thickness, and geometry that determine release kinetics — all directly applicable to GT2-printed drug delivery capsules and devices.

## IP-Visio Specific

Nanoscribe application note: IP-Visio resin — biocompatible photoresin for direct laser writing.
Available through the Nanoscribe customer portal.
