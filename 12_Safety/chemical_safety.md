# Chemical Safety

> The volumes of chemicals used in GT2 workflows are small — typically microliters of resist and a few milliliters of developer. Small volumes do not mean no hazard. Acrylate sensitization, solvent reproductive toxicity, and fire risk from IPA are all genuine concerns that are fully managed by following the procedures in this file.

---

## Chemicals Used in GT2 Workflows

| Chemical | Role | Primary Hazard |
|---|---|---|
| IP-Dip2, IP-S, IP-Q, IP-Visio, IP-PDMS, IPX-series | Photoresist | Skin/eye irritant; acrylate sensitizer |
| GP-Silica | Photoresist (glass precursor) | Skin/eye irritant; silica particle inhalation if dried |
| PGMEA (propylene glycol methyl ether acetate) | Primary developer solvent | Reproductive toxin (Category 2); skin/eye irritant; vapor inhalation |
| IPA (isopropanol) | Rinse solvent | Flammable (flash point 12 C); CNS depressant at high vapor concentration |
| Acetone | Substrate cleaning | Highly flammable (flash point -20 C); CNS depressant |
| SU-8 developer (if used with i-line resists) | Developer | Eye and respiratory irritant; flammable |

---

## IP-Series Resists — Acrylate Sensitization

IP-series resists (and most other 2PP photoresists) are based on **acrylate monomers**. Acrylates are chemical sensitizers — with repeated skin contact, some individuals develop an allergic sensitization response. Once sensitized, even trace skin contact can trigger a reaction ranging from contact dermatitis to systemic allergic response.

Sensitization is irreversible. There is no treatment that removes the sensitization once acquired. An individual who becomes sensitized to acrylates may be unable to work with these materials again.

**This is the primary reason for strict glove discipline with these resists, even though the volumes are small.**

Rules:
- Always wear nitrile gloves when handling any IP-series or IPX-series resist. Latex gloves are insufficient — acrylates permeate latex.
- Change gloves immediately if they contact resist. Do not touch anything else (doorknobs, keyboards, face) with contaminated gloves.
- If resist contacts skin, wash immediately with soap and water for at least 15 minutes.
- Do not handle resists if you have open cuts or abrasions on your hands.

---

## PGMEA — Reproductive Toxin

PGMEA is classified as a **Category 2 reproductive toxin** (may damage fertility or the unborn child) under GHS/CLP. This classification is based on animal studies. Direct human evidence is limited, but the precautionary principle applies.

Rules:
- Always work with PGMEA in a **fume hood**. Never develop samples at the open bench.
- Wear nitrile gloves. PGMEA penetrates skin slowly but measurably over extended contact.
- Do not pour PGMEA into open beakers and leave them unattended — vapor accumulates.
- Pregnant individuals or individuals planning pregnancy should consult their occupational health service before working with PGMEA. Many facilities have substitution policies for this situation.
- Dispose of PGMEA-containing waste as hazardous chemical waste per your facility's procedures. Do not pour down the drain.

---

## IPA — Fire and Vapor Hazard

IPA (isopropanol) has a flash point of 12 C, meaning it is flammable at room temperature. At typical usage volumes (a few mL for rinsing), the fire risk is low if basic precautions are followed.

Rules:
- Keep IPA away from open flames and hot plates.
- Do not accumulate large volumes of IPA-soaked materials (wipes, swabs) in an open waste container — use a sealed waste jar.
- Work in a well-ventilated area. IPA vapor at high concentrations causes dizziness and headache; the threshold for CNS effects is well above typical lab air concentrations with normal ventilation.
- Store IPA in a flammables cabinet when not in use.

---

## Acetone — Highly Flammable

Acetone is used for substrate cleaning. Its flash point (-20 C) means it is flammable even at cold temperatures.

Rules:
- Keep away from all ignition sources.
- Use the smallest volume necessary for cleaning.
- Work in a fume hood or well-ventilated area.
- Seal the container immediately after use.

---

## GP-Silica — Particle Inhalation Risk

GP-Silica is a liquid suspension during use. The hazard arises if the dried resist (before or after printing, if a sample is not properly handled) becomes airborne as a fine powder. Silica nanoparticles are a lung hazard when inhaled.

Rules:
- Never allow GP-Silica or GP-Silica-printed samples to dry in an uncontrolled way that could generate airborne particles.
- Handle dried brown bodies (after debinding) in a ventilated enclosure or while wearing a properly fitted N95 or P100 respirator.
- Do not sand, grind, or mechanically abrade GP-Silica-printed structures without respiratory protection.

---

## Fume Hood Use

A fume hood must be used for:
- All PGMEA development steps
- Any procedure using more than 5 mL of IPA
- Any procedure using acetone
- SU-8 developer handling
- GP-Silica brown body handling

**Fume hood operating rules:**
- Sash at or below the working line marked on the hood
- Verify airflow indicator is active before starting work
- Keep materials at least 15 cm behind the sash when working
- Do not block the baffle at the back of the hood
- Never put your head inside the fume hood

## Spill Procedures

**Small spill (< 10 mL, on bench or floor):**
1. Alert others in the area.
2. Put on nitrile gloves and safety glasses if not already wearing them.
3. Absorb the spill with paper towels or dry vermiculite.
4. Dispose of contaminated material in a sealed hazardous waste bag.
5. Wipe the area with IPA-dampened paper towels.
6. Dispose of IPA-contaminated wipes in the sealed waste bag.
7. Report the spill to your facility's chemical hygiene officer if PGMEA is involved.

**Large spill (> 10 mL, or any spill with potential for vapor accumulation):**
1. Evacuate the immediate area.
2. Activate your facility's chemical spill response procedure.
3. Do not re-enter until cleared by safety personnel.

**Skin contact with resist or PGMEA:**
1. Remove contaminated gloves.
2. Wash affected skin with soap and water for 15 minutes.
3. If irritation persists, seek medical attention and bring the SDS (Safety Data Sheet) for the chemical.

**Eye contact with any chemical:**
1. Immediately go to the eyewash station.
2. Flush with water for at least 15 minutes, holding eyelids open.
3. Seek medical attention.

---

## Waste Disposal

- PGMEA-containing waste: hazardous chemical waste — seal in labeled waste container
- IPA-containing waste: flammable hazardous waste — separate from PGMEA if your facility requires it
- Polymerized resist fragments: check with your facility — some can be disposed as solid waste, others require hazardous waste disposal
- GP-Silica waste: check with facility — silica nanoparticle waste may have specific disposal requirements

All waste must be labeled with contents, date, and your name. Never pour organic solvents down the drain.

---

## Safety Data Sheets

SDS documents for all chemicals used in GT2 workflows are available from Nanoscribe (for IP-series and GP-Silica resists) and from chemical suppliers (for PGMEA, IPA, acetone). Keep printed copies in the lab or ensure they are accessible digitally before starting any work involving these chemicals.
