# Module 12 — Safety

> The GT2 is a Class IV laser system combined with hazardous photochemicals. Neither hazard is dramatic under normal operation — the enclosure contains the laser, and the chemical volumes are small. But both hazards can cause serious irreversible injury if the procedures in this module are not followed.

---

## Who Must Read This Module

Every person who operates the GT2, prepares substrates, handles resists, or performs development must read this module before their first unsupervised session. This includes:

- New users completing initial training
- Visiting researchers and collaborators
- Students starting a project that uses the GT2
- Anyone returning after an extended absence from the system

This module does not replace facility-specific safety training. Your facility will have its own laser safety program, chemical hygiene plan, and emergency procedures. The content here supplements that training with GT2-specific information.

---

## The Two Primary Hazards

**1. Class IV femtosecond NIR laser**

The GT2 uses a 780 nm, 100 fs pulsed laser at 80 MHz repetition rate with an average power of up to approximately 200 mW at the sample. Class IV means the beam is capable of causing immediate, permanent eye injury from direct or specularly reflected exposure. The beam is invisible to the naked eye (NIR). You will not see it before it injures you.

The enclosure and SPS system contain this hazard during normal operation. The hazard is present any time the enclosure is open and the laser is active.

**2. Photochemical resists and solvents**

IP-series resists contain acrylate monomers that are skin and eye irritants and potential sensitizers. PGMEA (propylene glycol methyl ether acetate), the primary developer, is a reproductive toxin at high doses and a skin and respiratory irritant. IPA (isopropanol) is flammable.

These chemicals are used in small volumes (microliters to milliliters), which limits exposure risk. The hazard is managed by working in a fume hood and wearing appropriate PPE.

## Quick Reference: PPE by Task

| Task | Minimum PPE |
|---|---|
| Operating GT2 (enclosure closed, normal print) | Lab coat, closed-toe shoes |
| Opening enclosure hatch during laser operation | Never — laser must be off before opening |
| Handling liquid resist (IP-series) | Nitrile gloves, lab coat, safety glasses |
| Development in PGMEA | Nitrile gloves, lab coat, safety glasses, fume hood |
| IPA rinse | Nitrile gloves, lab coat, safety glasses |
| UV curing station | UV-blocking safety glasses or face shield |
| Furnace operation (GP-Silica sintering) | High-temperature gloves, face shield, lab coat |
