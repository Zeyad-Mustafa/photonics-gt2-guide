# 🌟 Section 00 — Introduction to the Nanoscribe Photonic Professional GT2

> **Learning Objective:** By the end of this section, you will understand what the GT2 is, what makes it different from a regular 3D printer, and what kind of structures it can create.

---

## What Is the Nanoscribe Photonic Professional GT2?

The **Nanoscribe Photonic Professional GT2** is a high-precision 3D printer that works at the **micro and nanoscale**. Unlike a desktop 3D printer that extrudes melted plastic in layers you can see with your eyes, the GT2 uses a **focused laser beam** to solidify a liquid material in spots smaller than a wavelength of light.

Its full technology name is **Two-Photon Polymerization (2PP)** — and it allows fabrication of structures with features as small as **160 nanometers** (0.00016 mm).

---

## Why Is This Machine Special?

| Feature | Regular 3D Printer | Nanoscribe GT2 |
|---|---|---|
| Smallest feature size | ~100–200 µm | ~160 nm |
| Technology | Extrusion / FDM | Two-Photon Polymerization |
| Printing medium | Solid filament | Liquid photoresin |
| Build volume | liters | ~100 × 100 × 8 mm |
| Precision | ±50–200 µm | ±10 nm (piezo mode) |
| Applications | Prototypes, objects | Optical devices, MEMS, microfluidics, biosystems |

---

## What Can It Make?

The GT2 is used to fabricate:
- 🔬 **Microoptical elements** — tiny lenses, beam shapers, diffraction gratings
- 💧 **Microfluidic chips** — channels for lab-on-chip devices
- 🦾 **MEMS structures** — micro actuators, cantilevers, sensors
- 🧬 **Biomedical scaffolds** — 3D structures for growing cells
- 🤖 **Microrobots** — sub-mm swimming or walking robots
- 💡 **Photonic crystals** — structures that control light at the nanoscale
