# ✅ 25× Objective — When to Use It

---

## Use the 25× When...

### ✅ Your smallest feature is between 1 µm and 50 µm
This is the 25×'s sweet spot. Features in this range print reliably, quickly, and with good surface quality.

### ✅ Your total structure fits within ~1 mm³
At 25× speeds, 1 mm³ takes several hours — still practical. Beyond that, consider the 10×.

### ✅ You need biocompatible materials (IP-Visio)
IP-Visio is only compatible with the 25×. For cell scaffolds, tissue engineering constructs, or any bio-facing device, the 25× is your objective.

### ✅ You need flexible/soft structures (IP-PDMS)
IP-PDMS is only compatible with the 25×. For microfluidic membranes, soft actuators, or elastic structures.

### ✅ You are printing microfluidic devices
Channel widths of 5–100 µm, heights of 10–200 µm — exactly what the 25× handles best.

### ✅ You are a first-time GT2 user
The 25× is the standard learning objective. More forgiving than the 63×, more precise than the 10×.

---

## Do NOT Use the 25× When...

### ❌ You need sub-micron features
Switch to 63×. The 25× voxel (~400 nm lateral) cannot reliably produce walls thinner than ~1 µm.

### ❌ Your structure exceeds 380 µm in height
The working distance of 380 µm is the hard limit. Use the 10× (700 µm WD) for taller structures.

### ❌ Your total volume exceeds ~1 mm³ and time is limited
A 5 mm³ structure on the 25× could take 2–3 days. The 10× would do it in hours.

### ❌ You need IP-Dip2 or IP-Q exclusively
IP-Dip2 requires the 63×. IP-Q works with both 10× and 25×, but is primarily the 10× resist.

---

## Quick Decision

```
Feature size 1–50 µm?          → ✅ 25× candidate
Volume < 1 mm³?                → ✅ 25× candidate  
Need IP-Visio or IP-PDMS?      → ✅ 25× only
Height < 380 µm?               → ✅ 25× candidate

All yes? → Use 25×
```
