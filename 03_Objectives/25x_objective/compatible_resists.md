# 🧪 25× Objective — Compatible Resists

---

## Compatible Resists

| Resist | Status | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **IP-S** | ✅ Primary | General research, low shrinkage, smooth surfaces |
| **IP-Visio** | ✅ Compatible | Biocompatible structures, low fluorescence |
| **IP-PDMS** | ✅ Compatible | Flexible, elastic, soft structures |
| **IP-Q** | ✅ Compatible | Large fast prints (also used with 10×) |
| **IPX-Clear** | ✅ Compatible | Highly transparent optical structures |
| **GP-Silica** | ✅ Compatible | Glass structures (requires special workflow) |
| IP-Dip2 | ❌ Not compatible | 63× only |
| i-line resists | ❌ Not compatible | 20× only |

---

## IP-S — The Default 25× Resist

**IP-S** is the standard resist for the 25× objective and the most commonly used resin in the GT2 ecosystem.

Key properties:
- Refractive index: n = 1.478 @ 780 nm
- **Low shrinkage (~5%)** — best dimensional accuracy of all IP resins
- Smooth surface finish after printing
- Good mechanical stiffness after curing
- Wide process window — forgiving of slight power/speed variations

Application: Drop cast 1–2 drops on ITO side of substrate, semi-spherical drop.

---

## IP-Visio — For Biomedical Applications

- Very low autofluorescence → compatible with fluorescence microscopy imaging
- Biocompatibility tested (ISO 10993 class)
- Transparent in visible spectrum
- Used for cell scaffolds, organ-on-chip, microfluidic bio-devices
- Same application method as IP-S

---

## IP-PDMS — For Flexible Structures

- Polydimethylsiloxane-based — soft, elastic, flexible after curing
- Shore hardness much lower than other IP resins
- Used for microfluidic membranes, soft actuators, flexible electronics substrates
- ⚠️ Requires longer development time than IP-S
- ⚠️ More sensitive to oxygen inhibition — ensure thorough UV post-cure
