# 🔧 10× Objective — Installation Steps

> Follow these steps exactly. Incorrect installation can damage the lens or your sample.

---

## Before You Begin — Safety Checklist

- [ ] Green "Process" light is **OFF**
- [ ] You have clicked **"Exchange Holder"** in NanoWrite
- [ ] You have clicked **"Open"** and waited for the button to flash
- [ ] Laser safety goggles are available (wear if enclosure lid is open)
- [ ] No resin is dripping from a previous objective

---

## Step-by-Step Installation

### Step 1 — Open the Enclosure
1. In NanoWrite: click **"Exchange Holder"** → click **"Open"** → wait for green flash
2. Gently lift the **top hatch lid**

### Step 2 — Remove Any Existing Objective
1. If another objective is installed, rotate it **counterclockwise** until it comes free
2. Place it in its **labeled protective case** immediately
3. Never set an objective down on a bench without its case

### Step 3 — Attach the Silicone Ring
The 10× uses a **silicone ring** (not a fiber ring — that is for the 63×):
1. Locate the silicone ring in the GT2 accessories box
2. Slide it onto the 10× objective body until it sits at the correct stop position
3. The ring prevents the objective from touching the substrate surface

### Step 4 — Install the 10× Objective
1. Hold the objective **by its body** — never touch the front glass element
2. Insert into the objective turret slot
3. Rotate **clockwise** until it stops — do NOT overtighten or force it
4. The objective should feel firmly seated but required no force to stop

### Step 5 — Select in NanoWrite
1. On the **touchscreen controller**: select the correct objective position
2. In **NanoWrite**: confirm objective is set to **"10×"** in the sample configuration dialog
3. Select holder type: **"Multi-DiLL Si Wafer"**

### Step 6 — Close and Resume
1. Gently lower the top hatch lid
2. In NanoWrite: click **"Close"**
3. The green "Process" light will come back on — the laser safety interlock is now active

---

## Removal Steps

Reverse the installation: Open → Click Exchange Holder → Open lid → Rotate objective counterclockwise → Remove → Store in case → Close lid.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Opening lid without clicking Exchange Holder | Piezo may be in raised position — objective could crash into substrate | Always click Exchange Holder first |
| Using fiber ring instead of silicone ring | Wrong standoff height — objective touches resin at wrong depth | Check ring type before installing |
| Overtightening the objective | Thread damage, difficult removal | Stop when rotation stops naturally |
| Selecting wrong objective in NanoWrite | Interface detection will fail | Confirm in software after hardware install |
| Touching front lens element | Optical contamination | Handle by body only |
