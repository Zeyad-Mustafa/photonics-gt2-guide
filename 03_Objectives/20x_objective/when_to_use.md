# ✅ 20× Objective — When to Use It

---

## Use the 20× When...

### ✅ You are doing 2D photolithography only
Flat patterns, gratings, alignment marks, electrodes — any structure that is essentially 2D.

### ✅ Your resist is spin-coated
Spin-coated resists form a very flat, uniform layer — perfect for the air-gap 20× exposure geometry.

### ✅ You are integrating with semiconductor fabrication
If your GT2 step is one layer in a larger microfabrication process that uses standard i-line resists, the 20× fits naturally into that workflow.

### ✅ You need large-area 2D patterning
With stitching, the 20× can pattern areas up to 100×100 mm — far larger than what is practical with DiLL objectives for 3D structures.

---

## Do NOT Use the 20× When...

### ❌ You need any Z-dimension structure
Even a 2 µm tall pillar requires DiLL mode. Use 25× or 63×.

### ❌ You are using IP-series resins
IP-Dip2, IP-S, IP-Q, IP-Visio, IP-PDMS — none of these are compatible with the 20×. They require DiLL immersion objectives.

### ❌ You need sub-micron resolution
Without immersion, the 20× air objective cannot achieve the tight focal volumes needed for sub-micron features.
