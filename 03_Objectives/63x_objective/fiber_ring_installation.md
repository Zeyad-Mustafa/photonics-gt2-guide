# 🔴 63× Objective — Fiber Ring Installation

---

## What Is the Fiber Ring?

The **fiber ring** is a thin loop of optical fiber that wraps around the front of the 63× objective tip. It serves two functions:

1. **Standoff spacer** — sets the correct distance between the objective front element and the substrate surface
2. **Illumination guide** — delivers light for the interface detection system (the fiber carries LED light that reflects off the substrate–resin interface)

The fiber ring is unique to the 63× objective. The 10× and 25× use a silicone ring instead.

---

## Fiber Ring vs Silicone Ring — Do Not Confuse Them

| Ring Type | Used With | Appearance |
|---|---|---|
| **Fiber ring** | 63× objective | Thin optical fiber loop, delicate |
| **Silicone ring** | 10× and 25× objectives | Soft rubber/silicone collar |

⚠️ **Never install a silicone ring on the 63×.** The standoff height will be wrong, the interface finder will not work, and you may damage the objective by crashing it into the substrate.

---

## How to Install the Fiber Ring

1. Locate the fiber ring in the GT2 accessories box — it is stored in a small protective case
2. Hold the 63× objective with the front element pointing up
3. Carefully slide the fiber ring over the front of the objective
4. Push gently until it seats at the correct position — it should sit at the marked groove or stop near the front element
5. The fiber loop should be centered and symmetric around the objective tip
6. Connect the fiber cable end to the interface detection port on the machine (if detachable — follow your lab's SOP)

---

## Fiber Ring Care

- The optical fiber is **fragile** — never bend it sharply or kink it
- After each session, inspect the fiber for cracks or damage
- If the fiber is broken: interface detection will fail — do not attempt to use the machine without a functioning fiber ring
- Clean resin off the fiber with lens tissue and IPA after each session
- Store in the protective case when not in use
