# Development Solvents

## Overview

The development solvent must dissolve uncured monomer efficiently while leaving the cured polymer intact. Different resins use different monomers and crosslinkers, so no single solvent is universally optimal for all resins. Using the wrong solvent — or the right solvent at the wrong concentration — is one of the most common causes of development failure.

---

## Solvent by Resin Type

### IP-Dip2

Primary developer: PGMEA (propylene glycol monomethyl ether acetate)
Development time: 15 to 20 minutes for structures up to 100 um tall
Rinse: isopropanol (IPA), 5 minutes

PGMEA is the standard and preferred developer for IP-Dip2. It dissolves the uncured monomer completely within the recommended time without causing measurable swelling of the cured polymer at room temperature.

Do not substitute acetone for PGMEA with IP-Dip2. Acetone develops too aggressively and can cause swelling or cracking of fine features.

---

### IP-S

Primary developer: PGMEA
Development time: 20 to 30 minutes for structures up to 200 um tall
Rinse: IPA, 5 minutes

IP-S uses the same development chemistry as IP-Dip2. The longer development time is due to IP-S being formulated with slightly different monomer ratios that are somewhat more resistant to dissolution in PGMEA.

---

### IP-Visio

Primary developer: PGMEA
Development time: 20 to 25 minutes
Rinse: IPA, 5 minutes

IP-Visio develops similarly to IP-S. Because IP-Visio is commonly used for biomedical scaffolds with complex internal geometry, ensure adequate development time to clear internal channels and pores. Gentle agitation (slow rocking, not ultrasonic) helps the solvent access internal features.

---

### IP-Q and IPX-Q

Primary developer: PGMEA
Development time: 20 to 30 minutes for large structures
Rinse: IPA, 5 minutes

IP-Q structures are typically larger than those printed with other resins. Larger structures contain more uncured resin and require longer development or refreshed solvent to fully develop. For structures over 1 mm in any dimension, consider a two-step development: first bath of PGMEA for 15 minutes, transfer to a second fresh bath of PGMEA for another 15 minutes, then IPA rinse.

---

### IP-PDMS

Primary developer: PGMEA
Development time: 25 to 35 minutes
Rinse: IPA, 5 minutes, then a second IPA rinse for 5 minutes

IP-PDMS is more resistant to development than other IP resins due to its silicone-based chemistry. A longer development time is required, and residual PDMS oligomers can remain on the surface after the first IPA rinse. A second IPA rinse is recommended. If the structure feels tacky after drying, it has not been fully developed — return to PGMEA for an additional 10 minutes.

---

### IPX-Clear

Primary developer: PGMEA
Development time: 15 to 25 minutes depending on structure volume
Rinse: IPA, 5 minutes

IPX-Clear develops similarly to IP-Dip2 and IP-S. For optical applications where surface cleanliness is critical, ensure fresh PGMEA and fresh IPA for the rinse step to avoid leaving any dissolved monomer residue on optical surfaces.

---

### GP-Silica

Primary developer: PGMEA
Development time: 20 to 25 minutes
Rinse: IPA, 5 minutes

Note: GP-Silica development removes the uncured organic binder around the silica nanoparticles. The developed structure at this stage is a fragile green body (a particle compact held together by the cured binder). Handle with extreme care — the green body is much more fragile than a fully cured IP-series polymer structure. Do not use any agitation. Submerge gently and leave undisturbed.

---

## PGMEA Properties and Safety

PGMEA (also written as PGMEA, 1-methoxy-2-propanol acetate, or propylene glycol monomethyl ether acetate) is a mild polar aprotic solvent.

Physical properties:
- Appearance: clear, colorless liquid
- Odor: mild, slightly sweet
- Boiling point: 146 degrees C
- Flash point: 42 degrees C (flammable — keep away from open flames and hot surfaces)
- Miscibility: miscible with most organic solvents, partially miscible with water

Safety:
- Irritant to skin and eyes — wear nitrile gloves and safety glasses
- Inhalation: mild irritant — use in a fume hood or well-ventilated area
- Not acutely toxic at typical exposure levels, but avoid prolonged skin contact
- Dispose of PGMEA waste in halogen-free organic waste containers — do not pour down the drain

Storage:
- Store in original sealed container at room temperature, away from heat sources and open flames
- Shelf life: 2 years from manufacture date if stored correctly

---

## IPA Properties and Safety

IPA (isopropanol, isopropyl alcohol, 2-propanol) is used as the rinse solvent after PGMEA development.

Why IPA is used as a rinse:
- IPA is miscible with PGMEA and flushes it from the structure surface and substrate
- IPA evaporates faster than PGMEA, giving a cleaner, faster dry
- IPA has lower surface tension than water, reducing collapse risk during drying
- IPA does not significantly swell or interact with cured IP-series polymers at room temperature

Safety:
- Flammable — flash point 12 degrees C — keep away from open flames
- Mild irritant — use gloves and work in ventilated area
- Dispose of IPA-PGMEA waste as organic solvent waste

---

## Solvent Freshness

Both PGMEA and IPA should be replaced regularly. Used development solvent accumulates dissolved monomer and loses effectiveness. As a practical guideline:

- Replace PGMEA after every 5 to 10 development sessions, or immediately if it appears cloudy or viscous
- Replace IPA rinse after every 3 to 5 sessions, or if it appears discolored
- For critical structures where surface cleanliness is paramount (optical elements), use fresh solvent for every session

Never return used solvent to the original container. Keep development waste in a dedicated labeled waste bottle.
