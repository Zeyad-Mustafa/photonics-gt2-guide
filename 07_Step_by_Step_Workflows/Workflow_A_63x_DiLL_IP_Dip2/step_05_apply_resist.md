# Step 5 — Apply IP-Dip2 Resist

> IP-Dip2 is a low-viscosity liquid. Applying the correct volume in the correct location, without introducing bubbles or contamination, takes practice. Read the full procedure before opening the resist bottle.

---

## Why This Step Matters

In DiLL (Dip-In Laser Lithography) mode, the 63x objective tip is submerged directly into the liquid IP-Dip2 during printing. The resist is both the printing medium and the immersion medium. If the drop is too small, the objective tip will not be fully submerged and the optical path will be disrupted. If the drop contains bubbles or contaminants, they will scatter the laser and ruin the print.

---

## Before Handling IP-Dip2

- Wear nitrile gloves. IP-Dip2 contains acrylate monomers that are skin sensitizers.
- Work in a low-light environment or under yellow/amber filtered lab lighting. IP-Dip2 is sensitive to UV and short-wavelength visible light. Do not work directly under fluorescent lights without UV filtering.
- Have your cleaned substrate ready and loaded in the holder (see step 07 — load the holder, then add resist).

Note: In practice, some operators apply resist to the substrate while it is still outside the holder (flat on the bench), then load the holder. Either order is acceptable. The key constraint is that the resist must be applied to the substrate surface that will face the objective — and applied just before printing, not hours in advance.

---

## Procedure

1. Remove the IP-Dip2 bottle from storage (4°C refrigerator or room temperature depending on your institution's storage protocol). Allow it to reach room temperature before use — approximately 15 minutes. Cold resist is more viscous and will not spread correctly.
2. Confirm the substrate is in the holder with the **printing surface facing down** (toward the objective, per the inverted microscope geometry).
3. Remove the cap from the IP-Dip2 bottle. Use the built-in dropper tip.
4. Tilt the bottle gently and allow **one drop** (approximately 5–10 uL) to form on the tip. Do not squeeze aggressively.
5. Place the drop on the **center of the substrate's printing surface**. The drop should sit as a small dome.
6. Visually inspect: the drop should be clear, bubble-free, and roughly hemispherical in shape.
7. If a bubble is visible in the drop, gently exhale across the surface (do not blow hard — this can contaminate the resist). Alternatively, use a clean pipette tip to carefully pop the bubble from the edge.
8. Cap the IP-Dip2 bottle immediately after use.
9. Load the sample holder into the stage using the Exchange Holder dialog (step 07).

---

## How Much Resist to Apply

For the 63x objective in DiLL mode, the resist drop volume is less critical than for the 10x (which requires a specific domed shape). The 63x objective tip enters from below, and the drop simply needs to be large enough to fully submerge the tip (approximately 2–3 mm diameter footprint is sufficient).

| Drop size | Result |
|---|---|
| Too small (< 3 uL) | Objective tip not submerged; optical contact fails; print fails |
| Correct (5–15 uL) | Objective tip fully submerged; clean interface detection |
| Too large (> 20 uL) | Resist may overflow the substrate edges; wastes material |

---

## What Can Go Wrong

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Bubbles in the resist | Air introduced during drop application | Let drop sit 1–2 min; bubbles rise; or use clean pipette to remove |
| Resist spreads too flat | Surface contamination or resist degraded | Re-clean substrate; check resist age and storage conditions |
| Resist solidifies before printing | Exposed to bright light or UV | Work in low-light; apply resist immediately before loading |
| Drop falls off when holder is inverted | Drop too small or surface energy too low | Apply slightly larger drop; ensure substrate is room temperature |
