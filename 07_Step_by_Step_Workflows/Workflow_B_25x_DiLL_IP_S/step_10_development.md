# Step 10 — Development

> Development for IP-S uses the same solvents as IP-Dip2 (PGMEA then IPA), but the timing differs slightly. IP-S is slightly more resistant to PGMEA than IP-Dip2 due to its higher crosslink density — standard development time is 20–25 minutes.

---

## Chemicals Required

| Chemical | Purpose |
|---|---|
| PGMEA | Primary developer |
| IPA | Rinse |
| N2 gun | Drying |

Work in a fume hood.

---

## Procedure

1. Use the Exchange Holder dialog to retract the stage. Remove the holder and extract the substrate.
2. Submerge the substrate (ITO/printing side down) in **PGMEA** for **20–25 minutes** with gentle agitation.
3. Transfer to **IPA** for a 5-minute rinse.
4. Dry with the N2 gun from center outward.
5. Inspect under an optical microscope.

Development time for IP-S may be slightly longer than for IP-Dip2 for structures of equivalent density. If residual resist haze is visible after 20 minutes, extend development to 30 minutes in fresh PGMEA.
