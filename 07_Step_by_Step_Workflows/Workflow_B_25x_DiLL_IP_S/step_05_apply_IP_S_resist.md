# Step 5 — Apply IP-S Resist

> IP-S is more viscous than IP-Dip2. It flows more slowly onto the substrate and holds a more stable dome shape. Application is straightforward, but the volume and timing still matter.

---

## Procedure

1. Remove IP-S from storage and allow it to reach room temperature (15 minutes).
2. Confirm the substrate is in the holder with the ITO surface facing down.
3. Apply **one to two drops** of IP-S to the center of the ITO surface (the surface that is currently facing up before the holder is inverted). A drop of approximately 10–20 uL is appropriate.
4. IP-S is more viscous than IP-Dip2 — it will not spread as readily. It should sit as a stable dome on the substrate.
5. Check for bubbles. IP-S can trap air bubbles more easily than IP-Dip2 due to its higher viscosity. If bubbles are visible, allow the drop to sit for 2–3 minutes — bubbles tend to rise and escape on their own.
6. Cap the IP-S bottle immediately.
7. Proceed to loading the holder (Step 7).

---

## Key Difference from IP-Dip2

IP-S has a refractive index of n = 1.478. When used in DiLL mode with the 25x objective, the objective tip is submerged directly in the IP-S. The 25x adjustment ring must be set to the "Glyc" (glycerol) position — three longest bars visible — to optically compensate for the IP-S immersion medium. See Step 6.
