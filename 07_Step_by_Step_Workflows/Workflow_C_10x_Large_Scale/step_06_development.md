# Step 6 — Development

> Development for IP-Q follows the same PGMEA then IPA procedure. Large structures printed with the 10x may require longer development times due to the greater resist volume.

---

## Procedure

1. Use Exchange Holder dialog to retract the stage. Remove the holder and extract the silicon substrate.
2. Submerge the substrate (polished/printed side down) in **PGMEA**. Development time: **20–30 minutes** for standard structures. For dense large structures (> 500 um in any dimension), use **30–45 minutes**.
3. Agitate gently — the larger resist volumes on 10x prints benefit from slightly more active agitation (a slow magnetic stir bar is ideal).
4. Transfer to **IPA** for 5 minutes.
5. Dry with N2.
6. Inspect under optical microscope.

IP-Q leaves less residual resist than IP-S because it is lower viscosity. Development times on the shorter end of the range are usually sufficient.

---

## UV Curing

UV post-curing follows the same procedure as Workflows A and B. Expose at 405 nm for 10–20 minutes.

Large structures printed in IP-Q may benefit from the full 20-minute cure to ensure complete crosslinking throughout the bulk volume.
