# Step 3 — Apply IP-Q Resist

> IP-Q application is the most critical and most easily misjudged step in Workflow C. One to two drops only. A semi-spherical dome shape is required. Read the full file before opening the bottle.

---

## The Drop Shape Rule

This is covered in full detail in [04_Resists_and_Materials/IP_Q/drop_casting_technique.md](../../04_Resists_and_Materials/IP_Q/drop_casting_technique.md). Read that file first if you have not already.

Summary:
- Apply **1–2 drops only** (approximately 10–20 uL total)
- The drop must form a **semi-spherical dome** — not a flat puddle
- A flat drop means surface contamination or too much resist was applied
- A flat drop will cause interface detection to fail

---

## Procedure

1. Confirm the silicon substrate is in the Multi-DiLL Silicon Wafer Holder with the **polished side facing up** (the resist will be applied to this face; after the holder is inverted into the stage, the polished side faces the objective).
2. Allow IP-Q to reach room temperature.
3. Apply 1 drop to the center of the polished substrate surface. Observe the drop shape.
4. If it forms a clear dome: proceed.
5. If it spreads flat immediately: remove the substrate, re-clean (acetone > IPA > DI water > N2), and retry.
6. A second drop may be applied adjacent to the first for larger print areas. They will merge into one slightly larger dome.
7. Cap the IP-Q bottle.

---

## What Can Go Wrong

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Drop spreads flat | Surface contamination | Re-clean substrate |
| Interface detection fails | Flat drop or drop too small | Remove substrate, re-clean, re-apply 1–2 drops with dome shape |
| Drop falls off when holder inverted | Drop too small or silicon has low surface energy | Ensure drop is large enough; retry |
