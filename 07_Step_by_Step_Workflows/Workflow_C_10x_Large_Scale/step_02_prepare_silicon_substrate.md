# Step 2 — Prepare the Silicon Substrate

> The 10x workflow uses silicon wafer pieces as the standard substrate. Silicon must be diced to the correct size, polished side identified, and cleaned before resist application.

---

## Why Silicon for the 10x?

Silicon has a high refractive index (n ~ 3.5 at 780 nm), which gives an extremely large delta-n with IP-Q (n = 1.48). This makes interface detection fast and reliable. Silicon is also flat, inexpensive, and available in large wafers that can be diced to any size.

The Multi-DiLL Silicon Wafer Holder accepts pieces up to approximately 25 x 25 mm.

---

## Procedure

1. If starting from a full wafer, dice it to the required size using a DISCO saw or diamond scribe. A 15 x 15 mm to 25 x 25 mm piece is typical. See [05_Substrates/silicon_wafers.md](../../05_Substrates/silicon_wafers.md) for dicing guidance.
2. Identify the polished side (mirror-smooth, reflective) and the rough back side (matte).
3. Clean the polished side: acetone soak 5 min → IPA soak 5 min → DI water rinse → N2 dry.
4. Inspect: the polished surface should be clean, uniform, and free of particles.
5. Optional oxygen plasma treatment: 2 min, 100 W, O2. Improves IP-Q adhesion.
6. Use within 30 minutes of cleaning.
